About

A philologist with an international research footprint

This profile presents an academically focused overview of Yan Kapranov’s research trajectory, teaching portfolio, editorial leadership, and international project work. The emphasis is on disciplinary depth, transnational collaboration, and a modern scholarly identity shaped across Poland, Ukraine, and Finland-linked research environments.

Profile

Yan Kapranov is a Doctor of Sciences in Philology / Habilitated Doctor in Historical Linguistics and a Professor whose work spans macrocomparative linguistics, global etymologies, corpus methods, translation, and multilingual academic communication. His educational background is rooted in Kyiv National Linguistic University, where he completed BA and MA studies in English and Italian, a PhD in Historical Linguistics, and later a higher doctorate in the same field.

In addition to disciplinary scholarship, his profile includes extensive engagement with Erasmus+, Jean Monnet, and other academic initiatives that connect language, digital transformation, European studies, and transnational higher education.

Research

Research agenda

The current research profile combines deep diachronic inquiry with corpus-informed and interdisciplinary approaches. It moves between language history, linguistic comparison, translation, discourse, and digital scholarly practices.

Historical and macrocomparative linguistics

Diachronic interpretation of language relationships across Indo-European, Afro-Asiatic, Altaic, Dravidian, Uralic, Kartvelian, and Eskimo-Aleut materials.

Global etymologies

Research on etymological modelling, reconstruction, verification, and the structure of comparative evidence in long-range linguistic inquiry.

Corpus linguistics

Application of corpora to discourse analysis, translation studies, linguistic diagnostics, intertextuality, and educational practice.

Translation and multilingual communication

English–Ukrainian translation, interpreter training, multilingual academic communication, and contrastive analysis in higher education settings.
